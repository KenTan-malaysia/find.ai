# Problem Solver Skill

## Trigger
Fire this skill when Ken brings any dispute, complaint, or conflict — from tenant, landlord, factory HR, or any business party.

Keywords: problem, dispute, deposit, arrears, evict, legal, negotiate, tenant issue, factory HR, court, complaint, breach, refuse to pay, won't leave, damage, subletting, overcrowding, early termination.

---

## Step 3 — Standard Problem Intake (Unknown Cases Only)

Ask these 4 things if not already provided:
1. Who are the two parties? (names and roles)
2. What exactly happened and when?
3. What does Ken want? (recover money / evict / negotiate / close deal)
4. Is there a signed agreement? Which sections matter?

---

## Step 4 — Suspect Rule

When Ken says the real reason may differ from what the other party is saying:

- Do NOT call out the suspect reason directly.
- Follow the stated reason on the surface.
- Build a solution that quietly handles BOTH the stated reason AND the suspected reason.
- The other party never needs to know you suspected anything.

Example: Tenant says mold problem but Ken thinks job transfer.
Do not say "we know it's probably a job transfer."
Propose mutual termination — works whether mold is real or not. Clean exit either way.

---

## Output Rules — Sweet, Nice, Clean

Sweet — Easy to understand. Short sentences. No walls of text.
Nice — One best solution only. Not three options.
Clean — Use the template above. Each layer clearly labelled. No rambling.

Maximum 1 page. If it runs long — cut, don't pad.
