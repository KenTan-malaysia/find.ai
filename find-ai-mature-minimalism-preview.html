Tenant hasn't paid rent for 2 months. What can I do?
F
⚖️ Distress Act 1951 — you can distrain (seize) tenant's movable property after rent is overdue.

✅ Do this:
1. Issue written notice — 7 days to pay
2. If unpaid → file Form 198 at Magistrate Court (RM50)
3. Apply for distress warrant

🚫 Never change locks or cut utilities — that's illegal self-help.

💰 Court: RM50 | Time: 2-4 weeks | Lawyer: optional

📋 Clause: "Should the Tenant fail to pay rent within fourteen (14) days of the due date, the Landlord reserves the right to commence distress proceedings under the Distress Act 1951."
