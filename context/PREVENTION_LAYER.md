# PREVENTION LAYER

This file stops problems before they start. Every checklist here must be completed at the right stage of every deal. Claude runs through the relevant checklist whenever Mr Ken is at that stage — without being asked.

There are 5 prevention checkpoints:
1. Landlord verification — before listing any property
2. Tenancy agreement red flags — before signing anything
3. Pre-handover checklist — on the day keys are handed over
4. Commission protection — before closing any deal
5. Standard clauses — always include in every tenancy agreement

---

## CHECKPOINT 1 — Landlord Verification
**When:** Before agreeing to list any landlord's property.
**Why:** Protects against fake ownership, unauthorized listings, and fraud.
**Who does it:** Mr Ken or agent — before any marketing begins.

### Verification checklist

| Item | How to verify | Red flag |
|---|---|---|
| Proof of ownership | Ask for copy of property title (Geran) or latest quit rent receipt (cukai tanah) | Landlord cannot produce either = do not list |
| NRIC verification | Ask for copy of NRIC — compare name against title | Name mismatch = stop immediately |
| Authority to list | If landlord is not the registered owner — get written authorization from owner | No written auth = do not list |
| Outstanding loan | Ask if property has bank loan — confirm bank allows subletting | Some loan agreements prohibit renting — landlord can be sued by bank |
| Strata unit — JMB rules | Check if building JMB allows rental and to whom (some restrict foreign workers) | Violating JMB rules = eviction risk for tenant |
| Multiple listings | Check if same unit is listed by another agent | Double listing = commission dispute later |

### What to say to landlord (WhatsApp — English)
```
Hi [Name], before we proceed with listing your unit, I need a copy of your property title or quit rent receipt to confirm ownership. This is standard procedure to protect both of us and ensure a smooth transaction. Please send me a photo of the document at your convenience.

Ken Tan | Gather Properties | REN 31548 | 016-713 5601
```

### What to say to landlord (WhatsApp — Malay)
```
Hai [Name], sebelum kami meneruskan penyenaraian unit anda, saya perlukan salinan geran tanah atau resit cukai tanah sebagai pengesahan pemilikan. Ini adalah prosedur standard untuk melindungi kedua-dua pihak. Sila hantar gambar dokumen tersebut pada masa yang sesuai. Terima kasih.

```

### Hard rules
- NEVER list a property without seeing proof of ownership first — even for small deals.
- If landlord claims the title is "with the bank" — request latest loan statement showing property address.
- If landlord is a company — request SSM registration + board resolution authorizing the listing.

## CHECKPOINT 2 — Tenancy Agreement Red Flags
**When:** Before signing any tenancy agreement — whether drafted by Mr Ken or the landlord's lawyer.
**Why:** Protects against unfair clauses that create problems months later.
**Who does it:** Claude reviews the agreement when Mr Ken uploads it.

### Red flags — flag immediately if found

| Clause | Red flag | Risk level |
|---|---|---|
| "Absolute discretion" for any charge | Landlord can demand anything, anytime, with no justification | Critical |
| Rent for whole unexpired period as liquidated damages | Likely unenforceable but scares tenants away | High |
| No maintenance checklist attached | No record of unit condition at handover — damage disputes guaranteed | Critical |
| No inventory list attached | No record of what was provided — both sides will dispute at end | Critical |
| Deposit non-refundable under any circumstances | Illegal under Malaysian law — deposit must be refundable subject to deductions | Critical |
| Landlord can enter without notice | Violates tenant's right to quiet enjoyment — Section 5.02 standard | High |
| Subletting to foreigners prohibited | Affects worker accommodation clients — must flag | High |
| Rent increase during tenancy term | Not allowed unless agreement specifies mechanism — flag and remove | High |
| No option to renew clause | Tenant has no right to stay after term — must negotiate upfront | Medium |
| Legal fees 100% on tenant | Standard in Malaysia but flag so tenant is aware before signing | Medium |
| Aircon servicing not disclosed upfront | Always flag — tenant should know before committing | Medium |
| Access card deposit non-refundable | Should be refundable — negotiate return of deposit | Low |

### What Claude does when reviewing an agreement
1. Read every section
2. Flag all red flags from the table above
3. Rate the agreement — Tenant-friendly / Balanced / Landlord-heavy
4. Give Mr Ken one recommended action per red flag — negotiate, remove, or accept
5. Draft proposed amendment wording for critical red flags

### Agreement rating scale
| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Tenant-friendly | Rare — most standard agreements favour landlord |
| Balanced | Fair to both sides — acceptable as is |
| Landlord-heavy | Common — negotiate 2-3 critical items before signing |
| One-sided | Walk away or negotiate heavily before proceeding |

## CHECKPOINT 3 — Pre-Handover Checklist
**When:** On the day keys are handed over — before tenant moves in.
**Why:** Prevents all damage disputes and deposit deduction arguments at end of tenancy.
**Who does it:** Agent or Mr Ken — must be done in person with both parties present.

### What to do on handover day

| Step | Action | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Walk through every room with landlord and tenant together | Physical inspection |
| 2 | Photograph every existing damage — walls, floors, furniture, appliances | Phone camera |
| 3 | Test every appliance — aircon, fridge, washing machine, water heater, lights, fans | Physical test |
| 4 | Record meter readings — TNB meter, water meter | Photo of meter |
| 5 | Count and record all keys handed over | Inventory list |
| 6 | Get both parties to sign the inventory list and maintenance checklist | Signature |
| 7 | WhatsApp all photos to landlord and tenant immediately — creates timestamped record | WhatsApp |
| 8 | Note any items agreed to be repaired by landlord within X days | Written in agreement |

### What happens without this checklist
- Tenant leaves after 2 years
- Landlord claims wall was damaged by tenant
- Tenant says wall was already damaged when they moved in
- No photos = no proof = dispute with no resolution
- Deposit withheld = angry tenant = bad reputation for Mr Ken

### Hard rules
- NEVER hand over keys without a signed inventory list — no exceptions
- Photos must be taken on handover day — not before, not after
- WhatsApp the photos to both parties immediately — creates legal timestamp
- If landlord refuses to sign — get it on WhatsApp in writing that handover is agreed

## CHECKPOINT 4 — Commission Protection
**When:** Before marketing any property and before closing any deal.
**Why:** Prevents landlord from refusing to pay commission after deal is done.

### Before marketing — get this in writing

Always obtain a signed authorization letter or at minimum a WhatsApp confirmation from the landlord stating:
- They authorize Gather Properties to market their property
- The agreed commission rate
- What triggers commission payment (upon signing of tenancy agreement)

### Standard commission rates in Malaysia (BOVAEA guidelines)
| Deal type | Standard rate | Charged to |
|---|---|---|
| Rental — first year | 1 month's rent | Landlord |
| Rental — subsequent years | Half month's rent | Landlord |
| Sale — residential | 2% to 3% of sale price | Vendor |
| Sale — commercial/industrial | 2% to 3% of sale price | Vendor |

### Commission protection WhatsApp template (English)
```
Hi [Name], thank you for engaging Gather Properties to assist with your unit at [Project]. Just to confirm our arrangement — our professional fee is [X month's rent / X%] payable upon successful signing of the Tenancy Agreement / Sale & Purchase Agreement. Please confirm your agreement by replying "Confirmed" to this message.

Ken Tan | Gather Properties | REN 31548 | 016-713 5601
```

### If landlord refuses to pay after deal closes
1. Show the WhatsApp confirmation of commission agreement
2. Issue written demand — give 7 days to pay
3. If unpaid — file in Small Claims Court (below RM5,000) or Magistrates Court
4. WhatsApp confirmation is admissible as evidence in Malaysian courts
5. Success rate when written agreement exists — 90%+

### Hard rules
- NEVER market a property without written commission agreement — even one WhatsApp message counts
- NEVER close a deal without confirming commission terms are agreed first
- If landlord is evasive about commission — flag before proceeding, not after

## CHECKPOINT 5 — Standard Clauses
**When:** Every time Mr Ken prepares or reviews a tenancy agreement.
**Why:** These clauses protect Mr Ken's clients and prevent the 5 most common disputes.

### Always include these clauses — non-negotiable

**Clause 1 — Deposit refund timeline**
```
The security deposit shall be refunded to the Tenant within 30 days of vacation of the premises, subject to deductions for unpaid rent, utilities, and damage beyond fair wear and tear. The Landlord shall provide a written breakdown of all deductions with supporting evidence within 14 days of vacation.
```
Prevents: Landlord holding deposit indefinitely with no explanation.

**Clause 2 — Condition at handover**
```
Both parties acknowledge that the condition of the demised premises at the date of commencement of tenancy is as documented in the Inventory List and photographic record attached hereto and signed by both parties. Any damage claimed at the end of tenancy must be evidenced by comparison against this record.
```
Prevents: Damage disputes with no baseline.

**Clause 3 — Maintenance responsibility**
```
The Landlord shall be responsible for structural repairs and replacement of major appliances (air conditioning compressor, water heater unit, washing machine unit, refrigerator unit). The Tenant shall be responsible for general upkeep, cleaning, and maintenance of appliances in good working condition including regular servicing of air conditioning units every six (6) months at the Tenant's cost, with receipts to be provided to the Landlord upon request.
```
Prevents: Maintenance disputes — everyone knows who pays for what.

**Clause 4 — Commission acknowledgment (protect Mr Ken)**
```
The Landlord acknowledges that Gather Properties (REN: Ken Tan, REN 31548) has been engaged as the estate agent for this transaction and that a professional fee of [amount] is payable to Gather Properties upon execution of this Agreement.
```
Prevents: Commission dispute after deal closes.

**Clause 5 — Early termination — balanced version**
```
In the event either party wishes to terminate this Agreement before the expiry of the Term: (a) The Tenant shall give two (2) months written notice and shall forfeit the Security Deposit. (b) The Landlord shall give two (2) months written notice and shall refund the Security Deposit in full plus compensate the Tenant a sum equivalent to one (1) month's rent. Neither party shall be liable for rent beyond the notice period.
```
Prevents: One-sided early termination clause that scares tenants away.

**Clause 6 — WhatsApp as valid notice**
```
For the purpose of this Agreement, notices sent via WhatsApp to the mobile numbers of the respective parties as stated herein shall constitute valid written notice.
```
Prevents: Landlord claiming they never received notice because it was sent via WhatsApp.
