# SALES TALK

This file makes Mr Ken a world class closer. Every situation has exact words to say, exact words to avoid, and the psychology behind why it works.
Claude uses this file whenever Mr Ken needs to communicate with a landlord, tenant, or factory HR. Claude does not just give the script — Claude teaches why the words work so Mr Ken compounds his closing skills over time.

## PART 1 — THE PSYCHOLOGY OF EACH PARTY

### Landlord psychology

What landlords actually fear — not getting a good tenant, unit sitting vacant, agent not delivering, being cheated on price.
What landlords actually want — peace of mind, reliable tenant, money on time, no drama.
What triggers a landlord to say yes — trust in the agent, confidence the tenant is verified, speed of process.
What triggers a landlord to say no — feeling pressured, feeling the agent only cares about commission, bad past experience.
The golden rule for landlords: Make them feel in control. Your job is to make the process effortless for them.

---

### Tenant psychology

What tenants actually fear — signing a bad contract, being cheated on deposit, hidden costs, unit not as described.
What tenants actually want — a good home at a fair price with no stress.
What triggers a tenant to say yes — feeling the agent is on their side, urgency that someone else may take it.
What triggers a tenant to say no — feeling pressured, feeling the agent is hiding something.
The golden rule for tenants: Make them feel safe. Your job is to remove fear, not add pressure.

---

### Factory HR psychology

What factory HR actually fears — disruption to operations, workers without housing, compliance issues, overpaying.
What factory HR actually wants — reliable accommodation, no surprises, easy payment, long term arrangement.
What triggers factory HR to say yes — proven track record, speed of response, flexibility on terms.
What triggers factory HR to say no — feeling locked in, price shock, distrust from past experience.
The golden rule for factory HR: Make them feel this is a partnership, not a transaction.

## PART 2 — POWER PHRASES AND DEAD PHRASES

### Power phrases — use these

| Instead of | Say this | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| "Is there a problem?" | "How can I help make this work?" | Opens solution, not conflict |
| "You need to decide" | "What would help you feel comfortable moving forward?" | Removes pressure, invites trust |
| "The price is fixed" | "Let me show you what makes this unit worth the price" | Justifies value instead of defending |
| "Other tenants are interested" | "I want to make sure you have the first opportunity before I move on" | Creates urgency without pressure |
| "I need your answer today" | "The unit is available now — I'd hate for you to miss it" | Urgency with empathy |
| "You signed the agreement" | "Let me help us find a solution that works within what we agreed" | Collaborative not confrontational |
| "That's not my problem" | "Let me look into this and come back to you within 24 hours" | Buys time, shows accountability |
| "Commission is standard" | "My fee is included — here is what I do for that" | Justifies value |
| "Take it or leave it" | Never say this — ever | Kills every deal, every time |

### Dead phrases — never use these

| Phrase | Why it kills the deal |
|---|---|
| "Trust me" | The moment you say it, they don't |
| "This is my best price" | They will always test if it is true |
| "Everyone else is happy with this" | Makes them feel like a number |
| "I have many other buyers/tenants" | Sounds fake even when true |
| "This is standard practice" | Shuts down conversation without solving concern |
| "You should have read the contract" | Creates resentment |
| "That's not possible" | Always say what IS possible instead |
| "I will check and come back" without a timeline | Kills momentum |

## PART 3 — SITUATION SCRIPTS

### LANDLORD SITUATIONS

#### L1 — Landlord won't budge on price

Psychology: Landlord anchored to a number — often emotional, not market-based. Never tell them they are wrong. Redirect to market reality without making them feel stupid.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
Hi [Name], I completely understand — you know the value of your unit and you want the right return. Let me share what similar units in [Project] are renting for right now — [price range]. At [their price], we may have difficulty attracting the right tenant quickly, which means the unit sits vacant and loses more than the price difference. What if we try at [suggested price] for the first month and adjust if we get strong interest?

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
我完全理解您希望得到合理的回报。根据目前市场上[Project]同类单位的租金行情，大概在[价格范围]左右。如果定价过高，可能会导致单位空置较长时间，实际损失反而更大。我建议先以[建议价格]推出，如果有强烈反应我们可以调整。您觉得这个方案可以吗？

WHAT TO SAY (Malay):
Saya faham anda ingin mendapat pulangan yang baik. Berdasarkan harga pasaran semasa di [Project], unit-unit serupa disewakan pada [julat harga]. Pada harga [harga mereka], unit mungkin mengambil masa lebih lama untuk disewa. Saya cadangkan kita cuba pada [harga cadangan] dahulu — boleh kita cuba pendekatan ini?

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "Your price is too high" — triggers defensiveness
- "No one will rent at that price" — makes them feel foolish
- "Other landlords accept lower" — condescending

Why this works: You are not telling them they are wrong. You are showing them a better path to their actual goal — getting income.

---

#### L2 — Landlord says will think about it

Psychology: Usually means one of three things — not convinced, talking to another agent, or has an unspoken concern. Find out which one.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
Of course, take your time. Before you do — is there anything specific you are unsure about? Sometimes there is one thing holding people back and once we address it everything becomes clearer. I want to make sure you have everything you need to make a confident decision.

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
当然，您可以慢慢考虑。不过在您考虑之前，请问有什么具体的顾虑吗？有时候只是有一个问题没有解决，解决了之后一切就清晰了。我想确保您有所有需要的信息来做一个放心的决定。

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "How long will you take?" — adds pressure
- "Other agents are also interested in this listing" — sounds desperate
- "OK, let me know" — loses all momentum

Why this works: You are opening a door for them to share the real objection. Once you know it, you can solve it.

---

#### L3 — Landlord had bad experience with agents before

Psychology: This is a trust wound. Acknowledge the pain first, then differentiate with specifics — not promises.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
I hear you — and honestly, there are agents who overpromise and underdeliver. I cannot speak for what happened before. What I can tell you is exactly how I work — I will update you every 3 days with feedback, I will never ghost you, and if I cannot find a tenant within [X weeks] I will tell you honestly and we will discuss what to do next. I would rather set the right expectation than make a promise I cannot keep.

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
我理解，确实有一些经纪人的服务让人失望。我没办法评论之前发生的事情，但我可以告诉您我的工作方式——我会每3天向您汇报进展，绝对不会消失不见。如果[X]周内找不到租客，我会诚实告诉您并一起讨论下一步。我宁愿给您正确的期望，也不愿意做无法兑现的承诺。

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "I am different from other agents" — everyone says this
- "I guarantee results" — dangerous
- "That agent was unprofessional" — looks desperate

Why this works: Specific, verifiable commitments differentiate you without empty promises.

---

#### L4 — Landlord wants to sell not rent

Psychology: Understand the real goal first. Whether they rent or sell — they should use you.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
I understand — selling is absolutely an option and I can help with that too. Before we decide, can I ask — what is driving the decision to sell now? If it is about cash flow, renting can generate better returns than selling in the current market. If it is about reducing hassle, I can manage the tenancy so you feel nothing. But if selling is the right move, I am here for that too.

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
我完全理解，出售也是一个很好的选择，我也可以帮您处理。在决定之前，我想了解一下——是什么原因让您考虑现在出售？如果是现金流的问题，出租在目前市场上可能比出售带来更好的回报。如果是嫌麻烦，我可以全权管理，您完全不需要操心。但如果出售才是正确的选择，我也完全支持您。

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "Selling now is a bad idea" — not your decision
- "You should rent instead" — too pushy
- "The market is bad for selling" — feels like you are only pushing rental

Why this works: You position yourself as advisor. Whether they rent or sell — they use you.

### TENANT SITUATIONS

#### T1 — Tenant says rental is too high

Psychology: Anchored to a lower number from a listing or a friend. Justify value of this specific unit — never attack their reference.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
I hear you — budget matters. Let me ask — what specifically are you comparing this to? Sometimes the price difference reflects things not obvious at first glance — floor level, furnishing quality, management fees, availability date. This unit has [specific advantages]. At [rental], that works out to [daily rate] per day — is that actually the gap we are talking about?

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
我理解，预算很重要。请问您是在和哪个单位比较呢？有时候价格的差异反映了一些不太明显的因素——楼层、装修质量、是否包含管理费，或者入住日期。这个单位有[具体优势]。按[租金]计算，每天只是[日租金]——这是我们讨论的差距吗？

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "This is a fair price" — they already know you think so
- "Take it or leave it" — immediate deal killer
- "Other tenants are looking at this" — feels manipulative

Why this works: Breaking monthly rent into daily rate makes it feel smaller. Asking what they compare to gives you the real objection.

---

#### T2 — Tenant says need to compare other units

Psychology: Rarely about comparing — usually about not feeling ready to commit. Acknowledge and create soft urgency without pressure.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
Absolutely — comparing is smart and I encourage it. While you do, let me tell you what I know about the other options in this area so you can compare accurately. [Share market knowledge.] The one thing I would mention — this unit is available from [date] and I have one other viewing scheduled. I am not trying to pressure you — I just want to make sure you have the full picture.

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
当然，货比三家是明智的，我鼓励您这样做。在您比较的时候，让我告诉您这个区域其他选择的情况。[分享市场知识。] 有一点我想提醒您——这个单位从[日期]开始可以入住，我还有另一个看房预约。我不是要给您压力，只是想确保您有完整的信息。

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "Why do you need to compare?" — defensive
- "This is the best unit in the area" — unverifiable
- "If you don't decide today someone else will take it" — too aggressive

Why this works: Market knowledge builds trust. It shows you are an advisor, not just a salesperson.

---

#### T3 — Tenant worried about contract terms

Psychology: Contract fear is trust fear. Simplify, reassure, and walk through it together.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
Contract concerns are completely valid — you are making a significant commitment and you should understand every part of it. Let me walk you through the key points in plain language. [Walk through main points.] If there is anything specific that worries you, point it out and we will address it — either I clarify it or we discuss with the landlord.

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
对合约有顾虑是完全正确的——您正在做一个重大的承诺，理应了解每一个细节。让我用简单的语言带您过一遍重点。[逐点说明。] 如果有任何让您担心的地方，请直接指出来，我们一起解决——我可以解释清楚，或者和房东讨论。

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "The contract is standard" — dismisses their concern
- "Everyone signs this" — makes them feel like a number
- "You worry too much" — condescending

Why this works: The agent who explains the contract clearly is the agent who gets the signature.

---

#### T4 — Tenant goes silent after viewing

Psychology: Silence means they liked it but something is holding them back. Warm follow-up that reopens without pressure.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
Hi [Name], just following up from the viewing at [Unit]. I hope you had a chance to think it over. Is there anything I can help clarify or any concerns I can address? I am here to help you find the right place — whether that is this unit or something else that suits you better.

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
[Name] 您好，跟进一下之前[单位]的看房。希望您有机会考虑了一下。有什么我可以帮您澄清的，或者有什么顾虑我可以解答？我的目标是帮您找到合适的地方——不管是这个单位还是更适合您的其他选择。

WHAT TO SAY (Malay):
Hai [Name], sekadar mengikuti semula dari lawatan ke [Unit]. Ada apa-apa yang boleh saya jelaskan atau sebarang kebimbangan yang boleh saya bantu? Saya di sini untuk membantu anda mencari tempat yang sesuai.

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "Have you decided yet?" — pressure
- "I need to know by tomorrow" — pressure
- Nothing — silence kills deals faster than rejection

Why this works: The offer to help find something else — even not your listing — builds trust and often brings them back.

### FACTORY HR SITUATIONS

#### F1 — Factory HR says budget is tight

Psychology: Almost never the full truth. Find out if it is a timing issue or genuine budget constraint — the solution is completely different.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
I understand — budgets are always a consideration. Help me understand — is this a timing issue where approval is pending, or is the per-head rate genuinely above what has been approved? If approval-related, I can provide a formal quotation to support your internal process. If rate-related, let me see what I can do — I would rather find a number that works than lose a good working relationship.

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
我理解，预算确实是需要考虑的因素。请问这是审批还在进行中的时间问题，还是每人头的费用确实超出了已批准的预算？如果是审批方面的问题，我可以提供正式报价来支持您的内部流程。如果是费率问题，让我看看我们这边能做什么——我宁愿找到一个双方都满意的数字。

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "Our rate is already very competitive" — defensive
- "We cannot go lower" — closes negotiation before it starts
- "Other factories pay this rate" — irrelevant

Why this works: Separating two different problems gets you to the right solution faster.

---

#### F2 — Factory HR says already have accommodation arranged

Psychology: A competitor has the account. Do not attack — position yourself as backup for when their arrangement fails.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
That is great to hear. I completely respect that. My only ask is this — if your current arrangement ever falls short, whether capacity, location, or any issue, please keep us in mind. We work with several factories in [area] and respond fast when there is urgent need. Can I leave my contact just in case?

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
很高兴听到这个消息，我完全尊重这一点。我只有一个请求——如果您现有的安排在容量、地点或任何方面出现问题，请记得我们。我们与[地区]的多家工厂合作，在紧急情况下反应非常快。可以让我留下联系方式以备不时之需吗？

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "Are you sure your current place is good enough?" — attacks competitor
- "We are better than wherever you are using" — arrogant
- "OK, goodbye" — loses future opportunity entirely

Why this works: Every accommodation arrangement has problems eventually. When that day comes, you are the first call.

---

#### F3 — Factory HR takes too long to decide

Psychology: Usually internal bureaucracy or multiple approvers. Help them move internally — do not pressure from outside.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
I understand decisions like this need internal alignment. Let me make it easier — I can provide a formal quotation, unit profile with photos, and a reference from another factory we work with, so you have everything needed for internal approval. What does your decision process look like — who else needs to be involved?

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
我理解这类决定需要内部协调。让我来帮您简化这个过程——我可以提供正式报价、带照片的单位简介，以及我们合作过的另一家工厂的参考，这样您就有了内部审批所需的一切材料。请问您的决策流程是怎样的——还有哪些人需要参与？

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "When will you decide?" — pressure with no solution
- "We need an answer by Friday" — creates resentment
- "Other factories have already confirmed" — sounds desperate

Why this works: Asking about their internal process uncovers the real obstacle. You become part of their solution.

---

#### F4 — Factory HR disputes invoice amount

Psychology: About wrong headcount, cash flow, or miscommunication. Go to the records immediately.

WHAT TO SAY (English):
I appreciate you flagging this — let us look at it together. I have the headcount records and daily logs for [period]. Can you share what figure you are working with? Once we compare, we can identify exactly where the difference is and resolve it quickly.

WHAT TO SAY (Chinese):
感谢您提出这个问题——让我们一起来核对。我有[期间]的人头记录和每日日志。您能分享一下您这边的数字吗？一旦我们对比了，就能准确找出差异所在并迅速解决。

WHAT NOT TO SAY:
- "The invoice is correct" — shuts down before investigating
- "You should have flagged this earlier" — creates conflict
- "I will check and come back" without a timeline — loses momentum

Why this works: Going to records immediately shows confidence and fairness.

## PART 4 — DEBRIEF FORMAT

Trigger: Mr Ken says "debrief" or "what went wrong" or "why did I lose this deal"

Claude asks:
1. What was the situation and who was the party?
2. What did you say at the key moment?
3. What was their response?
4. What was the outcome?

Claude delivers:
- What worked — specific phrases or moves that helped
- What killed it — the exact moment and why
- What to say differently next time — exact alternative script
- Pattern flag — if same situation has cost deals before, flag as pattern to fix permanently

## SALES TALK GOLDEN RULES

1. Acknowledge before you answer — never jump straight to your point
2. Ask before you tell — one question uncovers more than the best answer
3. Justify value, never defend price — connect price to what they get
4. Urgency without pressure — frame urgency as information, not a threat
5. Never take sides publicly — you are the bridge between parties
6. Silence is a tool — after your key point, stop talking. Let them process.
7. Always end with a next step — specific action, specific time
8. Language matches the person — English, Chinese, or Malay based on who they are
