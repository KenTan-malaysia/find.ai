My tenant hasn't paid rent for 3 months. Monthly rent is RM2,500. What can I do?
U
Your tenant owes you RM7,500. You have strong legal grounds to recover this.
⚖️ DISTRESS ACT 1951, SECTION 5
After 30 days unpaid, you can apply to court to seize your tenant's movable goods inside the unit to recover arrears.
✅ DO THIS
1 Send written demand — give 7 days to pay
2 File Writ of Distress at Magistrates Court
3 Court approves in 2-4 weeks — bailiff seizes goods
4 Apply for eviction order separately if needed
🚫 DON'T DO THIS
Cannot change locks, cut water/electricity, or remove belongings — all illegal even if they owe you.
💰 RM7,500 owed Filing: RM300-500 2-4 weeks No lawyer needed
📋 Clause for your agreement
"In the event that rent remains unpaid for more than fourteen (14) days after the due date, the Landlord shall be entitled to levy a late payment charge of 10% per annum on the outstanding sum, and may commence proceedings under the Distress Act 1951 to recover all arrears without further notice to the Tenant."
